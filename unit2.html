Unit 2 - Technology Systems

This unit explores how technology systems work, including hardware, software and networks.

Evidence

Technology Systems focuses on understanding how computer systems work and how hardware and software combine to perform different tasks. Learners explore the components inside a computer system, including processors, memory, storage devices, input and output devices, and operating systems. The unit also looks at how technology systems are designed to meet different user needs and improve performance. Through this unit, learners develop knowledge of installing, maintaining, and selecting appropriate hardware and software while understanding how technology is used in homes, businesses, education, and other industries. Learners also gain practical skills in evaluating systems, identifying suitable technology solutions, and understanding factors that affect system performance and reliability. This unit can lead to future careers in IT support, computer engineering, systems administration, hardware engineering, software development, network engineering, cybersecurity, and technical support roles. It also provides useful knowledge for progressing into further education and careers within the wider technology and computing sector.

Reflection

This unit provided a comprehensive overview of technology systems, including the components and interactions between hardware, software, and networks. I learned about the different types of hardware, such as input devices, output devices, and storage devices, as well as the various software applications and operating systems. Additionally, I gained an understanding of how networks function, including the concepts of IP addresses, protocols, and security measures. The coursework was engaging and informative, allowing me to develop a solid foundation in technology systems. Overall, I am excited to apply this knowledge in future units and projects.
